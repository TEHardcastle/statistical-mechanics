Co-ordinate Systems

This diagram demonstrates that collections of points (ie atoms) can be described with different co-ordinate systems. Here, two points, A and B, are simultaneously represented with two different systems.

The table on the left uses the x and y co-ordinates of each of the points. The table on the right uses the x and y co-ordinates of the centroid of the two points, and the magnitude (dA-B) and direction (θ) of the line between the two points. The direction of the line is defined as the anti-clockwise angle between the line and the x-axis if the line was shifted so point A sits on the origin.
